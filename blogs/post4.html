My Coding Journey

The Original Plan

I grew up in Louisiana and moved around to many states like Iowa, Washington State, and now Texas. I never knew what I wanted to do in life. I was one of those students that only did good in school because I felt it was an obligation for my family. I had many of my family members try to convince me to become a doctor or a pharmacist. I learned the hard way that it takes a lot of time and effort in school to become a doctor and I was not sure if I was going to like it. My aunt is a pharmacist and she was still dealing with student loans even after getting her masters degree. This intimidated me since during my time in school.

The Light Bulb

So after some time with my family on talking about career plans, my father told me about computer science. My parents never completed high school and they never experienced a college education. During this time, I was attending high school in Louisiana. I looked into computer science and I was ignorant. I did not realize that there was a program like this existed, especially at the university that I attended. So I decided to give it a shot and this was what I wanted to do.

The Greed

During my time attending the University of New Orleans, I was so passionate on attending courses. I had a full ride, I wanted to know how to program. I saw a couple of my classmates taking different fields like cyber security, AI, game dev, and etc. I wanted to do them all. I was so interested in everything that in the end, I never chose a concentration. If I was able to go back in time to change something about myself, it is to not be greedy and choose a concentration. Maybe then, I would have got many internships.

The Result

Fast forward to the present (as of making this in January 2024), I am a desktop support attempting to get back into my original career into software development. I am going to attempt to get certifications to make up the lack of experience and make myself stand out among the sea of resumes. I want to build and grow professionally. Therefore, I will do more LeetCode, freeCodeCamp, and The Odin Project.
